Lt. General Saad ElDin ElShazly

The man who engineered the successful capturing of the Bar-Lev line. The first (and only?) Arab victory against Israel.

Here's a time line of Lt. General ElShazly life:

  • 1922 - Born in Basyoun - Gharbiya
  • 1939 - He joined the Military Academy and was the youngest student in his class.
  • 1940 - He graduated from the Military College with the rank of a lieutenant, while a lieutenant, he was chosen to serve in the Royal Guard.
  • Participated in Second World War
  • 1954 to 1961 - Founder and Commander of the First Parachute Battalion in Egypt
  • Commander of the 75th Parachute Battalion during the Tripatire Aggression
  • 1960 to 1961 - Commander of the UAR Battalion in the United Nations Operation in the Congo.
  • 1961 to 1963 - London War Attach√©
  • 1965 to 1966 - Commander of the 1st Infantry Brigade participated in the Yemen War
  • 1967 to 1979 - Commander of the Special Forces (Parachutes and Thunderbolts)
  • 1970 to 1971 - Commander of the Red Sea Military Region
  • 1971 to 1973 - Chief of Staff of the Egyptian Armed Forces
  • 1971 to 1973 - Assistant Secretary General of the League of Arab States for Military Affairs
  • 1974 to 1975 - Egyptian Ambassador to Britain
  • 1975 to 1978 - Egyptian Ambassador to Portugal
  • Feb 11 2011 - death, which was same day as Mubarak Resgination
